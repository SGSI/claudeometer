What you get

A fuel gauge for your Claude limits.

No more guessing how much you have left. Claudeometer reads your live quota and turns it into something you can read in half a second.

Live quota, gradient color

The 5-hour window right in the menu bar, with a color that shifts smoothly from green to red as you climb — no hard jumps.

Burn rate & ETA

See how fast you're spending (+%/hr) and a forecast of when you'll hit the wall — turns a static gauge into a warning system.

24-hour pace graph

A sparkline of your recent consumption, built from history kept locally for 30 days. Spot your heavy hours at a glance.

Graduated alerts

Notifications at 50%, 75%, 90% (panic), and 100% of the 5-hour window — each fires once, so you're warned, not spammed.

Every quota at once

5-hour, 7-day all, Sonnet, Opus, OAuth apps, and extra-usage spend — all in one tidy popover.

Hot Claude Code sessions

See which of your local Claude Code sessions are burning the most tokens in the last five hours.

How it works

Local, private, no setup.

Claudeometer piggybacks on the login you already have. Your token and usage data never leave your Mac — there's no account and no telemetry.

Reads your existing login

It uses the OAuth token Claude Code already stores in your macOS Keychain. Nothing new to sign into.

Checks your quota

It calls Anthropic's usage endpoint for your live numbers and keeps a small 30-day history locally for the pace graph.

Keeps you in the loop

The menu bar updates itself, and you get a nudge at each threshold — so a hit limit is never a surprise.

Install macOS 13+

Homebrew — recommended; opens with no Gatekeeper warning:

brew tap SGSI/claudeometer brew trust sgsi/claudeometer brew install --cask claudeometer

Or download the .dmg — drag Claudeometer to Applications. It isn't notarized yet, so clear the quarantine flag once:

xattr -dr com.apple.quarantine /Applications/Claudeometer.app
